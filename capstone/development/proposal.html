Capstone Proposal: Floating Cities

My topic of choice for the capstone project is floating cities, or urban developments built on a body of water. Though many people may see this concept as being high tech and futuristic, there are modern projects attempting to achieve this vision. However, there have been several examples of floating cities being constructed throughout history. The Uros indigenous people, for example, live on manmade islands in Lake Titicaca constructed from tightly woven totora reeds, while the Aztecs living on their island city Tenochtitlan expanded their land by constructing small artificial islands called chinampas that could support agricultural growth. This topic mainly relies on the lens of a utopian/dystopian narrative based around the consequences of climate change and our adaptations to it, though it would also challenge existing mental models on what human civilizations can be.

The content of my website would transport my users into a scenario where many people have been displaced due to climate change’s rising sea levels, but floating cities have been constructed in their place. This would play out like an interactive game. I was considering having the user choose between different kinds of floating architecture (traditional vs. modern vs. futuristic) to explore different options, with a “tour guide” character helping frame these decisions. For implementation of interactions and JavaScript libraries, I could have my users use drag & drop functions (JQuery, InteractJS, etc.) to build their own floating home. I was also considering having users submitting their own floating city concepts using p5.js. For more aesthetic purposes, I was planning to implement animated gradients and animate-on-scroll as well.

When initially considering this grand topic, I was concerned about how feasible this idea would be in real life. However, I’ve decided that if further research on this topic reveals obstacles such as weather, costs, or sustainability, I’ll address them the best I can; I’m not trying to present floating cities as a 100% perfect concept. My target audience will be teenagers and young adults, members of the current and next generations, because they’ll likely be the ones who’ll have to deal with climate change’s effects and craft solutions to respond. I imagine this could be provided to students in science classes or otherwise interested in STEM. I especially want those who live in cities and communities at risk of being displaced by rising sea levels to be users of my project; I want to give them some inspiration for the future.
